# Chehia — Product-fit audit (2026-07-15)

_7 adversarial lenses over the whole platform (code-grounded, file:line evidence in each finding). Generated pre-resubmission at the founder's request: 'will Tunisian venues and customers find this perfect?'_

## Lens: staff-accountability

**Verdict.** The accept-gate exists in shape — orders enter as 'new' and someone must press Accepter before the kitchen timer starts, and the migrations even document "nothing is prepared until a staff member accepts" — but the accountability half of the founder's vision is entirely missing: no status transition anywhere records WHO acted (no accepted_by/served_by/cancelled_by, no audit table, a bare `update({status})` from the client), the kitchen board itself shows unaccepted 'new' orders with a Start button so the waiter-verifies-at-the-table step can be silently skipped, and there is no cancel button or cancellation reason anywhere in the staff UI. After a dispute, an owner can reconstruct WHEN each step happened but never who confirmed a fake order — the exact question the founder wants answerable. The primitives to close this (per-staff accounts, staff_shifts, PIN infra, payments attribution) already exist; what's needed is an attributed transition RPC + append-only status-event log, a kitchen view gated on accepted orders, and a cancel-with-reason flow — roughly one to two weeks of focused work.

**Strengths (evidence-backed):**
- The staff-accept gate exists by design: orders enter as 'new' and only advance via new→preparing→ready→served with cancel allowed from new/preparing (packages/shared/src/status.ts:6-12), and the design intent is explicit — "nothing is prepared until a staff member accepts" (supabase/migrations/20260705000005_order_abuse_controls.sql:11-13).
- Every transition is server-timestamped by a DB trigger — accepted_at/ready_at/served_at/cancelled_at stamped in stamp_order_status (supabase/migrations/20260701000001_core_schema.sql:270-289) — so the WHEN of a dispute is always reconstructible, and clients cannot backdate (timestamps set in trigger context, coalesced once).
- Money paths are fully staff-attributed: payments.created_by_staff (supabase/migrations/20260710000001_pos_money_fiscal.sql:113), cash_sessions.opened_by/closed_by (:76,79), orders.created_by_staff for POS-originated orders (:22, register_order_tx :241-246), and refunds/discounts schemas already carry approved_by_staff (:152,166-167).
- Client writes to orders are column-scoped to `status` only — after an adversarial pre-prod review, UPDATE was revoked and re-granted on status alone so staff cannot forge paid_at/fiscal_number/total (supabase/migrations/20260710000004_pos_review_fixes.sql:21-25); RLS confines reads/updates to the venue's own staff and customers are select-only (supabase/migrations/20260701000001_core_schema.sql:380-392).
- Per-person staff identity primitives exist: owner/manager can mint individual waiter/kitchen/manager accounts server-side (supabase/functions/create-staff/index.ts:1-9,26-52), a staff_shifts time-clock with one-open-shift-per-staff enforcement (supabase/migrations/20260710000003_staff_shifts.sql:8-21) is wired into the Caisse (apps/web/src/app/caisse/caisse-provider.tsx:172-174), and a bcrypt register PIN protects the till (supabase/migrations/20260710000002_staff_pin.sql:8-44).
- The anti-fake-order perimeter is real: 5-open-orders cap per customer (supabase/migrations/20260702000001_atomic_orders_and_metrics.sql:59-63), per-session and per-table rate limits returning 429 (supabase/functions/place-order/index.ts:246-268), orders.origin distinguishes scan vs browse with a visible 'remote order' badge on the floor board (supabase/migrations/20260705000005_order_abuse_controls.sql:16-22; apps/web/src/app/business/orders/page.tsx:244-248), plus per-venue require_qr and server-enforced geofencing for remote orders (supabase/migrations/20260711000001_location_gating.sql:17-22).
- The live boards give a coherent operational picture: floor view with per-table urgency + waiter calls (apps/web/src/app/business/orders/page.tsx:24-32,122-147) and a glare-optimized kitchen display with late-ticket escalation at 8 min (apps/web/src/app/business/kitchen/page.tsx:11,112), both realtime via Supabase subscriptions (apps/web/src/app/business/use-live-orders.ts:133-170).

**Gaps:**
- **[BLOCKER · days] No actor recorded on any order status transition — 'who confirmed this fake order?' is unanswerable**
  - The single write path for every staff status change is a bare `update({ status })` with no actor, no reason, no log: apps/web/src/app/business/use-live-orders.ts:181. The orders table has accepted_at/served_at/cancelled_at but no accepted_by/served_by/cancelled_by (supabase/migrations/20260701000001_core_schema.sql:180-183), stamp_order_status stamps only timestamps (:270-289), and nothing anywhere writes to the public.events table (grep across supabase/ and apps/ finds zero inserts) — there is no audit trail of transitions at all. After a bad or fake order reaches the kitchen, the owner can prove when it was accepted but never by whom; responsibility can never fall on the staff member who confirmed it, which is the exact center of the founder's vision. Postgres even offers the actor for free (auth.uid() in the trigger), but it is not captured.
  - _Fix:_ Minimal design: (1) add accepted_by/served_by/cancelled_by uuid references public.staff to orders; (2) create an append-only order_status_events table (order_id, from_status, to_status, staff_id, reason, created_at) with staff-read RLS and no update/delete; (3) replace the client's direct UPDATE with a SECURITY DEFINER RPC advance_order_status(p_order_id, p_to, p_reason default null) that resolves the caller's staff row via auth.uid(), validates the transition server-side, stamps the *_by column, and inserts the event row; then revoke the update(status) grant from 20260710000004 so the RPC is the only path. One migration + swapping one function in use-live-orders.ts.
- **[HIGH · days] Kitchen board sees and accepts unverified 'new' orders — the waiter-verification step can be skipped entirely and cannot be required**
  - The kitchen display's first column is status 'new' with a full-width Start button that flips new→preparing directly (apps/web/src/app/business/kitchen/page.tsx:26,174-181). The founder's model — waiter walks to the table, verifies the order is real, THEN the kitchen prepares — has no enforcement point: kitchen staff (role 'kitchen') can accept any customer order, including geofenced remote/browse orders, without anyone visiting the table. There is also no venue setting to require table-side confirmation (restaurants has require_qr and require_location but nothing like require_table_confirmation — supabase/migrations/20260705000005_order_abuse_controls.sql:17, 20260711000001_location_gating.sql:17). For a Tunisian café worried about prank orders placed on table QRs, the only 'verification' is whichever screen is glanced at first.
  - _Fix:_ Add restaurants.require_table_confirmation boolean (default off to preserve current behavior). When on: the kitchen board filters to status in ('preparing','ready') only, and the accept action (new→preparing) is restricted in the advance_order_status RPC to roles owner/manager/waiter — kitchen role gets 'forbidden' for that transition. The floor/orders page (already waiter-oriented) keeps the Accepter button. Optionally auto-accept POS orders (created_by_staff not null) since the cashier rang them in person.
- **[HIGH · days] No way for staff to cancel an order — no cancel button, no void, no refund UI, and no cancellation reason field**
  - The DB allows new/preparing→cancelled (packages/shared/src/status.ts:7-8) and customers see a cancelled state (apps/web/src/app/r/_venue/order-screen.tsx:309), but no staff surface can produce it: the kitchen ticket offers only Start/Ready/Served (apps/web/src/app/business/kitchen/page.tsx:174-198), the floor card only Accepter/Servi (apps/web/src/app/business/orders/page.tsx:290-306), and the Caisse has zero void/annul/refund actions (grep of apps/web/src/app/caisse finds none — the refunds and order_discounts tables from 20260710000001_pos_money_fiscal.sql:143-172 have no write path anywhere: read-only RLS and no RPC inserts). Faced with a fake or wrong order, staff must either leave it rotting on the board or mark it served — which counts it into todayRevenue (apps/web/src/app/business/use-live-orders.ts:122-128) and corrupts the venue's daily numbers. There is also no orders.cancel_reason column, so even a raw-API cancellation carries no explanation for a later dispute.
  - _Fix:_ Add a cancel action on new/preparing tickets (both boards) that requires picking a reason (customer_left / fake_order / out_of_stock / input_error / other+note) and routes through the same advance_order_status RPC so the canceller and reason land in order_status_events and orders.cancelled_by. Defer the full refund/void UI, but stop advertising the refunds table in Z-reports until it is writable (reports.tsx shows refunds_total that can only ever be 0).
- **[HIGH · hours] Legal transitions are enforced only in the client — any staff account can rewrite order history via the API**
  - canTransition lives solely in packages/shared/src/status.ts:14-16 and is consulted only by UI code; the DB grant update(status) to authenticated (supabase/migrations/20260710000004_pos_review_fixes.sql:25) plus the row-scoped policy (20260701000001_core_schema.sql:383-385) lets any active staff — including kitchen role — set ANY status on ANY of the venue's orders via PostgREST: served→new to re-fire a paid ticket, served→cancelled after settling to shrink reported revenue, or cancelled→served. stamp_order_status coalesces existing timestamps (core_schema.sql:277-280) so replayed transitions keep the old stamps and the record becomes incoherent. Combined with the missing actor attribution, a dishonest waiter can quietly cancel served cash orders and pocket the payment with no trace tying the change to them — the classic POS fraud the founder's accountability model is meant to prevent.
  - _Fix:_ Enforce the transition matrix server-side: either extend stamp_order_status to `raise exception 'illegal_transition'` when the (old,new) pair is not in the whitelist, or fold it into the advance_order_status RPC from the blocker fix and revoke direct UPDATE entirely. Add `if old.paid_at is not null and new.status = 'cancelled' then require manager role` as a guard for post-payment voids.
- **[HIGH · days] One shared login on the register/kitchen tablet collapses everyone into one identity — PIN infra can't tell waiters apart**
  - verify_my_pin only verifies the signed-in auth user's own PIN (supabase/migrations/20260710000002_staff_pin.sql:29-44); the lock screen is an unlock pad for that single account with no staff picker (apps/web/src/app/caisse/_register/lock-screen.tsx:7-14). In the realistic Tunisian deployment — one tablet at the counter, one screen in the kitchen, both signed into one account all day — every sale, clock-in (staff_shifts keys off auth.uid(), 20260710000003_staff_shifts.sql:35), and (future) order confirmation is attributed to the same staff row no matter which of five waiters touched it. Per-person accounts exist (create-staff), but expecting each waiter to carry a personal device or re-login mid-rush is not how cafés operate; without on-device staff switching, the accountability columns from the blocker fix would record the tablet, not the person.
  - _Fix:_ Add PIN-based staff switching on the shared register: extend the lock screen with a staff picker (venue roster is already readable under 'staff read own profile', core_schema.sql:330-331), add verify_staff_pin(p_staff_id, p_pin) returning a short-lived acting-staff assertion, keep 'acting staff' in provider state, and pass it as the actor to placeOrder/settleOrder/advance_order_status. Auto-relock after N minutes idle. This is the standard POS pattern (waiter code per action) and is what makes 'responsibility falls on who confirmed it' real in a one-tablet venue.
- **[MEDIUM · hours] Customer is told 'sent to the kitchen' while the order is still unconfirmed**
  - For status 'new' the customer tracking screen shows "Reçue — transmise à la cuisine" / "Received — sent to the kitchen" / "تم الاستلام — أُرسل إلى المطبخ" (packages/shared/src/i18n/fr.ts:149-150, en.ts:151-152, ar.ts:151-152; rendered at apps/web/src/app/r/_venue/order-screen.tsx:428-429). Under the founder's model nothing has gone to the kitchen yet — a waiter still has to verify at the table. This mis-shapes expectations both ways: customers start their mental prep-timer before acceptance, and if the venue rejects a fake/mistaken order the customer was already told the kitchen had it, making the cancellation feel like a broken promise rather than a declined request.
  - _Fix:_ Change the 'new' step copy across fr/ar/en to 'Envoyée — en attente de confirmation du serveur' (waiting for staff confirmation), and flip to 'en cuisine' only at 'preparing' (trackingStep already distinguishes them, packages/shared/src/status.ts:33-46). Three i18n edits plus the mobile app which shares the same strings.
- **[MEDIUM · hours] waiter_calls.acknowledged_by exists but is never written — table-call accountability silently dead**
  - The schema anticipates attribution — acknowledged_by uuid references staff(id) (supabase/migrations/20260701000001_core_schema.sql:218) and it even got an index in the 2026-07-13 hardening pass (20260713044846_advisor_hardening_perf.sql:116) — but the only acknowledge path writes just status and acknowledged_at (apps/web/src/app/business/use-live-orders.ts:192-195). When a customer later complains 'I called for the bill and nobody came, yet the app says handled', the owner cannot see which staffer pressed Handled. Same one-shared-login caveat applies.
  - _Fix:_ Resolve the caller's staff.id once in useLiveOrders (select id from staff where auth_uid = auth.uid()) and include acknowledged_by in the update — or route through the same attributed-action RPC family for consistency. Two-line change plus the staff-id lookup.
- **[MEDIUM · days] No owner-facing order history / dispute-reconstruction view**
  - The portal shows only open orders (use-live-orders.ts:80 filters status in new/preparing/ready) and the kitchen board the same; once served or cancelled an order vanishes from every staff surface. The Caisse Z-report aggregates payments and sessions without staff names (apps/web/src/app/business/caisse/reports.tsx:54-58 selects no opened_by/closed_by; stats page is pure aggregates). Even after attribution lands in the DB, an owner investigating 'table 7 says they never ordered this' has no screen to find the order, see its timeline (placed 14:02 by anon customer via scan → accepted 14:04 by Ahmed → cancelled 14:10 by Sana, reason: fake_order) — they would need SQL access. Disputes are exactly when a non-technical Tunisian owner needs this most.
  - _Fix:_ Add a simple /business/history page: date-picker + table filter over orders (all statuses) joining order_status_events and staff display names, rendering a per-order timeline. Read-only, one query per order, reuses existing RLS. Ship after the attribution migration since it is the payoff view that makes the whole accountability story visible to the owner.

## Lens: anti-abuse

**Verdict.** On the exploitation lens, Chehia is defensively well-designed for a cash-at-counter product: prices are recomputed server-side, every customer write goes through one gated edge function, reviews require a served-and-owned order plus manual moderation, and there are real rate/burst/open-order caps. The two things that keep abuse from being catastrophic are structural: nothing is prepared until staff tap "accept", and there is no in-app money, so the worst outcome is wasted staff attention, not theft or food loss. That said, it is NOT "nothing exploitable." The biggest hole is that a photograph of a table's QR is a permanent, unrevocable ordering capability that skips the geofence entirely, and the owner has no self-service way to rotate a token, block a table, or pause ordering when abuse starts. The geofence is honestly labelled in-code as spoofable, and anonymous-auth has no captcha and no cleanup, so every per-user cap is resettable by minting a fresh anonymous user. None of these block App Store resubmission, but a motivated troll in a Tunis café can absolutely disrupt one venue today.

**Strengths (evidence-backed):**
- Prices are always recomputed server-side from the DB and client prices ignored, with modifier min/max and negative-total guards — no price tampering possible (supabase/functions/place-order/index.ts:311-380).
- qr_token is a 128-bit random secret (encode(gen_random_bytes(16),'hex')) and is NOT enumerable: the old public-read-tables policy was replaced by a token-scoped resolve_table RPC, and list_venue_tables never returns the token (supabase/migrations/20260701000001_core_schema.sql:154; 20260703000001_secure_table_resolve.sql:11-27; 20260705000001_discovery_and_geo.sql:36-48).
- Real order rate limits exist and are layered: per-user 6 orders / 5 min (place-order/index.ts:248-256), per-table 4 orders / 90 s (place-order/index.ts:260-270), a 5-open-order cap enforced atomically in place_order_tx (20260709000002_group_ordering.sql:424-429), plus lines/order <= 50 and qty 1-20 (place-order/index.ts:180-187).
- Review bombing is strongly gated: a review requires an order that is SERVED, owned by the caller, and inside the platform window; one review per (order,item) via a unique index; reviews land 'pending' for admin moderation by default (submit-review/index.ts:82-134; 20260707000001_reviews.sql:39,224-234).
- Owner recourse on reviews is correctly constrained — a DB-level trigger lets owners ONLY hide reviews, never approve/edit/inflate them, closing an obvious rating-manipulation path (20260707000001_reviews.sql:153-184).
- Waiter-call spam is tightly bounded: a race-safe unique partial index allows exactly one open call per table, plus a 5-open-calls-per-customer cap (20260702000001_atomic_orders_and_metrics.sql:19-20; call-waiter/index.ts:64-70).
- Group sessions are membership-scoped by RLS, cap active participants at 12, use unambiguous 6-char codes (~729M combos), auto-expire after 6 h, and now delete a leaver's cart lines so they can't ride along in the host's order (20260709000002_group_ordering.sql:282,147-155; 20260715211228_group_session_hygiene.sql).
- A genuine venue kill switch exists: require_qr disables the entire remote/browse path so only scanned-QR orders are accepted, and it is owner-toggleable in Settings (20260705000005_order_abuse_controls.sql:16-17; apps/web/src/app/business/settings/page.tsx:238).
- Privileged provisioning functions (admin-provision-business, create-staff) keep gateway JWT verification ON as defense-in-depth, and place_order_tx / place_review_tx have execute revoked from anon/authenticated so only the service role can call them (supabase/config.toml:416-420; 20260709000002_group_ordering.sql:479-482).

**Gaps:**
- **[HIGH · days] A photographed QR is a permanent, unrevocable order-from-anywhere capability — and the owner cannot rotate the token or block the table**
  - The scanned flow treats holding the qr_token as proof of presence and explicitly SKIPS the geofence (place-order/index.ts:213 sets origin='scan'; the location gate at :226-243 only runs for origin==='browse'). qr_token is a permanent column default that never rotates (20260701000001_core_schema.sql:154), so anyone who once photographed the table card — or received the photo in a WhatsApp group, or found the venue's own 'test' link — can place orders to that exact table from home indefinitely, and require_qr does NOT help because the photo IS the QR. The natural remediation (deactivate that table / regenerate its token) does not exist in the portal: tables/page.tsx only supports ADD, print, and test — there is no deactivate, delete, or regenerate-token action, and the list is even filtered to is_active=true so a DB-disabled table just vanishes (apps/web/src/app/business/tables/page.tsx:32-73). The only backstops are the staff-accept gate and the per-table 4/90s burst cap, so impact is wasted staff attention and possibly prepared food if staff accept blindly, not money — but the owner is powerless to stop a specific leaked table.
  - _Fix:_ Add owner-facing table controls that already have DB support: a 'deactivate table' toggle (tables.is_active exists) and a 'regenerate QR' action (UPDATE tables SET qr_token = encode(gen_random_bytes(16),'hex')) exposed via an owner/manager RPC. Reprint replaces the leaked card. Optionally show origin='browse' vs 'scan' on the orders board so staff can be skeptical of off-site orders.
- **[HIGH · hours] The remote geofence is fully spoofable and the browse flow is open by default — fake off-site orders to any table are feasible out of the box**
  - customer_lat/customer_lng/customer_accuracy_m are read straight from the request JSON and trusted (place-order/index.ts:232-243); the code itself admits this is 'self-reported and therefore spoofable' (:224). Anyone calling the edge function directly (or using a mock-GPS app) just sends coordinates on the venue pin. Meanwhile table_ids are enumerable for any active venue via list_venue_tables(slug) (20260705000001_discovery_and_geo.sql:36-48), so an attacker needs no QR at all for the browse path. Defaults make this the out-of-box posture: require_qr defaults FALSE (20260705000005_order_abuse_controls.sql:17) and require_location defaults TRUE (20260711000001_location_gating.sql:17) but is the spoofable check. So by default a scripted client can place browse-flow orders to any enumerated table from anywhere. Impact is again bounded by staff-accept + pay-at-counter + the per-table 4/90s cap, so it is disruption, not theft.
  - _Fix:_ Default new venues to require_qr=true (scanned-only) so the spoofable path is off unless a venue deliberately opts into remote ordering; make the Settings copy state plainly that location gating deters casual abuse but is not a hard guarantee. Cheap add-on: also rate-limit browse orders per source IP (x-forwarded-for) in place-order, not just per anon user.
- **[MEDIUM · days] Anonymous sign-in has no captcha and no cleanup, so every per-user cap is resettable and auth.users grows unbounded**
  - enable_anonymous_sign_ins=true with anonymous_users capped at 30/hr/IP and NO captcha configured (auth.captcha is commented out) (supabase/config.toml:165,179,191). All the strongest limits key on created_by = the anon user id: per-user 6 orders/5min (place-order/index.ts:248-256), 5-open-order cap (20260709000002_group_ordering.sql:424-429), 5-open-waiter-calls (call-waiter/index.ts:64-70), and one-review-per-order. An attacker who mints a fresh anonymous user (trivially, and across rotating IPs) resets every one of these. There is also no scheduled prune of anonymous users — the only staged cron is inventory-alerts + generate-insights (20260713000003_schedule_nightly_functions.sql.staged) — so junk auth.users rows accumulate forever (a slow cost/DB-bloat vector). Only the per-table caps, which are identity-independent, hold the line.
  - _Fix:_ Turn on Turnstile/hCaptcha for anonymous sign-in (the config slot already exists) at least in prod, and schedule a nightly job to delete anonymous users older than N days with zero orders/reviews. Keep the per-table caps as the identity-independent backstop.
- **[MEDIUM · hours] No owner 'pause ordering' or per-table brake — the incident-response control that would blunt every abuse above is missing**
  - restaurants.is_active is checked server-side (place-order/index.ts:207 restaurant_inactive) and tables.is_active is checked too, but NEITHER is toggleable from the business portal: Settings exposes require_qr, require_location, reviews_enabled and inventory toggles but no is_active/pause switch (apps/web/src/app/business/settings/page.tsx:98,235-254), and the tables page has no per-table disable (tables/page.tsx). So during a spam wave, a rush, or a leaked-QR incident, an owner cannot instantly stop incoming orders themselves — they must call support to flip is_active in the DB. This missing brake is what turns the QR-replay and geofence-spoofing issues from 'annoying' into 'no way to make it stop tonight'.
  - _Fix:_ Wire two toggles to the existing columns: a venue-level 'Pause ordering' (restaurants.is_active, or a dedicated ordering_paused flag so it doesn't also hide the venue from discovery) and a per-table active toggle, both owner/manager-gated. This is a few hours since the server-side checks and RLS already exist.
- **[LOW · hours] Group-session cart griefing and a >50-line placement DoS**
  - A session member (present at the table, or holding the 6-char share code) can add lines to the SHARED cart with no per-participant line cap; qty is capped at 20/line but the number of lines is not (session_cart_lines has only the qty check — 20260709000002_group_ordering.sql:51-61). In group mode the host places ALL session lines, so a malicious member inflates the group order (mitigated by the everyone-ready gate + host review at place-order/index.ts:141-151). Worse, place-order caps total lines at 50 and checks it AFTER loading the session cart (place-order/index.ts:180), so a griefer who adds 51+ lines makes every group placement fail with too_many_lines — blocking the whole table from ever placing its group order. Requires membership (physical presence or the code), so real-world likelihood is low, but it is a clean griefing DoS on the group feature.
  - _Fix:_ Add a per-participant line cap (e.g. 20 lines) in session_cart_lines insert RLS or the cart RPC, and have group placement gracefully cap/trim rather than hard-fail at 50 lines so one member can't wedge the table's order.
- **[LOW · hours] Global 'auto' review mode would remove the human moderation gate platform-wide**
  - Reviews default to manual moderation and land 'pending' (submit-review/index.ts:73,134), which is the main defense against review bombing. But platform_reviews_config.moderation_mode can be set to 'auto', in which case submit-review stamps reviews 'approved' immediately and they count toward public averages with no human check (submit-review/index.ts:134; 20260707000001_reviews.sql:224). It is admin-only (RLS restricts the singleton to platform admins) and off by default, so this is a latent risk rather than an open one — worth flagging so nobody flips it to reduce moderation workload without re-adding a throttle. Note reviews still require a real served, owned order, so even 'auto' can't be spammed without genuine orders.
  - _Fix:_ If 'auto' is ever enabled, pair it with a per-venue/day approved-review cap or keep comments in manual mode while auto-approving star-only ratings. Otherwise leave manual as the default and document the trade-off.

## Lens: owner-day1

**Verdict.** The owner journey is unusually deep for a pre-launch product — a real 7-step onboarding wizard, AI photo menu import, printable branded QR cards, a Tunisia-specific fiscal POS (matricule fiscal, TVA, timbre, gap-free numbering, Z-reports) with an offline queue — and a founder-assisted pilot venue can genuinely run week 1 on it. But it is built for hand-holding, not autonomy: signup is admin-provision-only, there is no password recovery, no staff offboarding, no order cancel/refund, no in-product support channel, and two features the UI advertises (nightly AI insights, low-stock alerts) are not scheduled in prod so they never fire. Verdict: viable for a founder-run pilot of a handful of venues; not yet shaped for a venue to go live or survive week 1 without the founder as the helpdesk.

**Strengths (evidence-backed):**

## Lens: tunisia-customer-fit

**Verdict.** Chehia's Tunisia fit is genuinely strong where it counts: cash-at-counter reassurance is stated in culturally exact copy at the two anxiety points, the trilingual system is type-enforced and complete with correct Western-digit د.ت pricing and RTL, and the group-order/call-waiter/bill mechanics map well onto family café habits. The three real holes for this market are (1) no opening-hours truth anywhere — customers can order into a closed café and see "ouvert jusqu'à 22:00" at midnight, which is exactly the Ramadan/late-night pattern Tunisian cafés live on; (2) raw up-to-5MB menu photos served untransformed to metered 3G data plans; and (3) no Android app in a ~85-90% Android market — mitigated by the QR-web-first design but a gap for discovery/retention. The value prop over shouting for a waiter is real (menu+prices upfront, no waiting, l'addition in one tap); the two additions that would clinch it for a Tunis regular are a one-tap "reorder my usual" from the last served order and honest per-venue wait times instead of the hardcoded 8-minute countdown.

- **[HIGH · days] Dead-lettered offline sales are invisible — collected cash with no record and no recovery UI**
  - Offline sales the server permanently rejects (4xx) or that exhaust 8 attempts move to the 'failed-sales' IndexedDB store (apps/web/src/app/caisse/_register/offline-queue.ts:96-99, caisse-provider.tsx:482-511). The register banner shows only a count (register.tsx:48-57); no screen lists them, no retry, no export, no discard. The cashier physically took the money; the sale is now dead data pinned behind a permanent warning badge, invisible to Z-reports and the owner. Device-bound IndexedDB also means a stolen/broken tablet takes pending and failed sales with it. Documented verified as backlog #17 (ROAD-TO-LAUNCH.md:387-391), unshipped.
  - _Fix:_ Add a 'Ventes non synchronisées' sheet opened from the banner: list lines/total/reason per failed sale with retry / print copy / discard-with-confirmation. Expose allFailed()/removeFailed() from offline-queue.ts. Cheap insurance for the exact trust moment (money already in the drawer).
- **[MEDIUM · days] Offline sales that sync after the drawer closes post to the wrong (or no) cash session**
  - settle_order_tx attaches the payment to whichever session is open AT SYNC TIME (pos_money_fiscal.sql:343-346), not the session during which the cash was collected. Realistic sequence: Wi-Fi dies at 20:00, five cash sales queue, cashier counts and closes the drawer at 23:00 (close succeeds only if the network returned — the close RPC itself has no offline path, caisse-provider.tsx:447-459), queue drains at 23:10 → payments land with cash_session_id NULL (or tomorrow's session). Tonight's Z is 'over' by the queued cash, tomorrow's is 'short' or polluted. The daily variance number — the one feature meant to build owner trust in the POS — is wrong in both directions.
  - _Fix:_ Stamp the open session id (and the sale timestamp) into PendingSale at queue time and pass it through settle-order → settle_order_tx to post to the originating session even if closed (or to an explicit 'hors session' bucket surfaced in reports). Also block or warn on closing the drawer while pendingCount > 0.
- **[MEDIUM · days] No kitchen ticket printing — QR orders reach the kitchen only via a live screen**
  - The only ESC/POS output is the customer payment receipt after settle at the register (tender-sheet.tsx:119-127, escpos.ts, receipt.tsx; thermal-print.ts is WebUSB = Chrome/Edge only, explicitly not iOS Safari per its header comment). Nothing prints when an order is placed — not for QR orders (no surface even could: the caisse doesn't see them) and not a kitchen copy for register orders. Tunisian kitchens overwhelmingly run on paper tickets; the alternative offered (a dedicated always-on kitchen display, business/kitchen/page.tsx) demands a spare screen, a browser session that never sleeps, and constant connectivity. Without either a printed ticket or a robust display, 'accepted' orders will be forgotten in the kitchen during rush.
  - _Fix:_ Reuse the existing ESC/POS pipeline: an encodeKitchenTicket() (items, table, note, big font — no prices) auto-printed from the caisse/portal when an order is accepted, with a manual 'print ticket' button on order cards as the ship-now version. Auto-print on arrival needs the caisse to subscribe to incoming orders — which gap #2 requires anyway.
- **[MEDIUM · hours] Abandoned orders never expire — no no-show handling and (with no cancel) they permanently pollute the day**
  - A customer who orders and walks out leaves an order in 'new' forever: no cron exists (the staged nightly migration only schedules inventory-alerts and insights, supabase/migrations/20260713000003_schedule_nightly_functions.sql.staged), the kitchen's 'late' escalation applies only to 'preparing' tickets (kitchen/page.tsx:11, 112), and unaccepted 'new' orders just pulse. The floor plan marks the table occupied indefinitely (orders/page.tsx:90-118), todayCount/todayRevenue count it as revenue (use-live-orders.ts:122-128), and the customer-side open-order cap (5 per anon user, 20260702000001_atomic_orders_and_metrics.sql:60-64) can even lock a legitimate returning customer out days later since their anon session persists. Staff's only cleanup is lying ('served').
  - _Fix:_ Once gap #1 lands, add a visual escalation on 'new' orders older than ~15 min with a one-tap cancel ('client parti ?'), and a nightly pg_cron that auto-cancels orders stuck in 'new' for >6h so boards and stats self-heal.
- **[MEDIUM · hours] Group order dies with a generic error when one member's item sells out mid-flow**
  - place-order validates the whole session cart and rejects everything with item_unavailable naming only a UUID (place-order/index.ts:289-291). group-cart.tsx maps not_ready / host_only / session_closed but everything else — including item_unavailable — falls through to t.errors.generic (apps/web/src/app/r/_venue/group/group-cart.tsx:79-84), and unlike the solo cart (cart-screen.tsx:80-83) nothing reconciles the session_cart_lines. The host of an 8-person table gets 'Something went wrong', retries forever, and cannot tell which of 15 lines is the dead one. Mid-rush this is exactly when it happens.
  - _Fix:_ Map item_unavailable in group-cart to a specific message, and on that code refetch items + delete (or badge) session cart lines whose item is now unavailable so the group can re-place immediately. Server could also return the offending item_id/name in the error payload.
- **[MEDIUM · weeks] POS is pay-first only — no held orders / open tabs for table service**
  - The tender sheet places AND settles in one atomic flow (tender-sheet.tsx:71-77); there is no way to fire a 'sur place' order to the kitchen and collect payment after the meal — the only 'tab' is the single in-memory ticket, lost on tab close and unable to coexist with a second table's ticket. Combined with gap #2 (no settle UI for existing orders) a restaurant doing classic service physically cannot use the caisse as designed: they must demand payment at order time, which is not how sit-down dining works in Tunisia. Cafés (pay-at-counter) are fine; restaurants are not.
  - _Fix:_ The settle-existing-orders UI from gap #2 doubles as the minimal tab system: place the order unpaid via placeOrder (already possible — settle is a separate call), let it live on the orders board, and settle it later from the caisse's open-orders list. Defer true multi-ticket hold/recall.
- **[LOW · days] Shift handover is honor-system: venue-wide drawer, any waiter can open/close, no blind count**
  - One open cash session per venue (unique index, pos_money_fiscal.sql:92-93) opened/closed by any active owner/manager/waiter (open_cash_session role check, pos_money_fiscal.sql:394-396). Handover mid-day means close-Z + reopen — workable — but nothing forces a count at handover, nothing attributes sales between cashiers within a session (payments.created_by_staff exists but no per-cashier report reads it), the counted-cash input shows expected cash on the same screen before counting (cash-drawer.tsx:74-99, classic skim enabler), and cash_movements (paid_in/paid_out/drop) has no write path or UI at all despite being subtracted in the close formula (pos_money_fiscal.sql:439-442) — so buying change or paying a supplier from the till guarantees a false variance.
  - _Fix:_ Ship-now: a paid-in/paid-out dialog in the cash drawer writing through a small guarded RPC (the close math already handles it — the table is just unreachable). Later: blind count (hide expected until counted is entered) and a per-cashier breakdown from payments.created_by_staff.
- **[LOW · hours] Browse flow: one tap on a number grid picks the table — no confirmation, no staff-side fix**
  - The table picker commits on a single tap of a bare label grid (table-picker.tsx:70-73; same pattern on mobile); '12' vs '21' at 60px apart is an easy on-site mistake the geofence cannot catch (customer is legitimately inside). Staff see only a small 'Remote' badge (orders/page.tsx:244-248) with no cue that the table might be wrong, and there is no UI to move an order to another table — the runner just delivers to the wrong table and the two parties sort it out. Low frequency, but each occurrence burns both the customer and staff trust in remote orders specifically.
  - _Fix:_ Cart already re-states the table (cart-screen header); add a one-line confirm on the place button for browse orders ('Table 12 — Terrasse, correct?'). Staff-side 'move to table' (a plain orders.table_id update permitted by existing RLS) is an hour of UI.

## Lens: competitive-completeness

**Verdict.** Chehia's core loop — trilingual QR menu, live ordering, waiter paging, group ordering, reviews, AI insights, and a Tunisia-fiscal offline POS — is genuinely competitive with Sunday/me&u/Qlub-class products and better localized (millimes, timbre fiscal, forfait/réel, d17 tender, Arabic RTL). What's missing is the retention and revenue layer every comparable pitch includes: no loyalty, no promotions engine (the AI insights literally tell owners to "Créer la promotion" with a dead button), no customer-facing receipt/history, and no venue contact/social/maps presence despite lat/lng already being stored. None of these block resubmission; the top two or three are what will decide whether venues renew after the first month.

- **[HIGH · hours] TOP-5 #1 — Venue contact card: tap-to-call, WhatsApp, Instagram, directions (hours of work, disproportionate payoff)**

- **[HIGH · weeks] TOP-5 #2 — No promotions / happy-hour engine, and the AI insights point at one that doesn't exist**

  - Zero loyalty machinery exists (grep for loyalty/stamps/points across apps+supabase matches only timestamp helpers).   - _Fix:_ Encode ReceiptData to ESC/POS and ship one pragmatic transport first (Epson ePOS-Print/network printer or WebUSB), with the browser-print path kept as fallback.

Competitors ship a receipt link by default, and a digital reçu is a tangible artifact venues can point to when a customer disputes a total.
  - _Fix:_ After settle_order_tx stamps fiscal_number/paid_at (already realtime-pushed to the order row), render a receipt view on the customer order screen with fiscal number, TVA/timbre lines and a share/save action; add a lightweight local 'past orders' list keyed to the anonymous user.
- **[MEDIUM · hours] Orders can be placed while the venue is closed — opening hours are display-only**

A browse-flow customer at 1am can fire an order into an empty kitchen; the venue discovers a stale 'new' order in the morning and blames the app.
  - _Fix:_ Enforce opening_hours in place-order (venue-timezone aware, using the existing timezone column) with a clear 'closed, opens at HH:MM' error, and grey out the CTA client-side.
- **[MEDIUM · days] No split payment at the counter — schema anticipates it, RPC can't do it**
